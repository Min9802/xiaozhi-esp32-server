# Open Letter to Developers

"The spring river warms, the ducks are first to know, just when the pufferfish want to swim upstream!"

Dear friend, I am John, an ordinary Java programmer in a regular company. Today, with utmost sincerity, I'm sending this open letter to you who love AI technology and innovation.

Half a year ago, I saw many excellent projects, such as `Dify`, `Chat2DB` and other artificial intelligence related projects. I was thinking, how wonderful it would be if I could participate in these projects, but unfortunately "having no way to serve the country, coding in vain for ten years".

I discovered Xiage's team's videos in early 2025, and I was very curious about how they implemented it. I wanted to replicate their backend service and build a low-cost civilian Jarvis. Unfortunately, what I've created so far is still just artificial stupidity - low concurrency, no soul, slow response, and many bugs.

Xiage's team is our learning target. I really want to have a Xiaozhi backend service as intelligent as Xiage's team. But I can also understand Xiage's decision not to open source. "A single flower does not make spring, but a hundred flowers in bloom bring spring to the garden." The era when artificial intelligence blooms everywhere may be realized in our generation. We can use our own hands to implement low-cost civilian Jarvis. I personally believe that what they can achieve, we can also achieve - it's just a matter of time. I call it "our journey to obtain the scriptures."

So on this journey to obtain the scriptures, what difficulties will we encounter? I think there will be no fewer than eighty-one trials. Along the way, there will inevitably be various monsters, but there will also be immortals secretly helping us, and people joining our scripture-seeking team.

If you find the above content amusing, then I feel very fortunate. Being able to make you laugh for five seconds in your life of more than 30,000 days can also be considered a contribution I've made to you.

Will this idea of civilian low-cost Jarvis fail? I don't know, but in the lives of us ordinary people, isn't such failure quite common?

One thing is certain about the future: someone will definitely fully replicate Xiage team's functionality and implement civilian low-cost Jarvis. Will this project be us?

Looking forward to working with you hand in hand to create the future.

John, March 11, 2025, Guangzhou

# Appendix: Development Contribution Guide
## Project Goals

1. **Civilian Low-Cost Jarvis Solution**  

2. **Intelligent Interconnection with Peripheral Hardware Solution**  

## Join Us

We warmly welcome like-minded friends to join and contribute to the project together. You can check the features we plan to implement in the near future at [this link](https://github.com/users/xinnan-tech/projects/3). Features in the list that have not yet been assigned to relevant personnel are exactly where your participation is urgently needed. Participation methods are as follows:

### 1. Become a Regular Contributor

Fork the project, submit PR, which will be reviewed by developers and merged into the main branch.

### 2. Become a Developer

After you have submitted 3 valid PRs cumulatively, you can contact the group owner to apply to become a developer. The group owner will invite you to join the independent developer group to discuss the project's future together.

## Developer Development Process

1. **Create New Branch**  
   Please develop each feature point using a new branch approach. Branch names should be concise and clear, allowing people to see the implemented functionality at a glance, avoiding feature conflicts.

2. **Submit PR for Review**  
   After feature development is completed, please submit a PR on GitHub, which will be reviewed by other developers and merged into the main branch after approval.
